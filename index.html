What is happening in my world

Tropical Forest Monitoring and Assessment Service

The IGAD Climate Prediction and Applications Centre (ICPAC), under the Global Monitoring for Environment and Security & Africa (GMES & Africa) project, implemented Earth Observation based System for Tropical Forest Surveillance, Monitoring and Assessment (TFSMA) Service. The application’s main aim was to use Earth Observation methods/algorithms and tools to generate geospatial data products for tropical forest monitoring. The specific objectives was to establish forest coverage using EO data (Sentinel 1,2) using EO algorithms, detect changes using Radar and assess the carbon emission or enhanceed in the Eastern Africa forests

Land Degradation Assessment - Land Susceptibility to wind and water Erosion

The project implemented in patnership with Regional Center for Mapping of Resource for Development (RCMRD), assessed the vulnerability of Eastern Africa to Land Degradation, while modelling around five climatic, soil physical and chemical properties, and vegetation variables to assess the vulnerabilities of these areas. The machine learning modelling culminated into a web service development where users can request various variables and recive the visualised layers

Spatial Temporal Mapping of Water Hyancinth in Lake Victoria

Water hyacinth (Eichhornia crassipes) is an invasive hydro plant that invaded the waters of Lake Victoria and has since been spreading rapidly affecting the socio-economic livelihood of the community around the Lake. The weed's rapid spread is due to various anthropogenic activities in the surrounding environment among them being the eutrophication of the lake waters.
